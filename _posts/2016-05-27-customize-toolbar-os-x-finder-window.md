* To start customizing your Finder window’s toolbar, follow these quick steps:
* 1) Open a finder window by clicking on the Finder icon in your Mac’s Dock.
![img](http://media.idownloadblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/New-Finder-Window-on-Mac.png)
* 2) Right-click somewhere in the toolbar, and in the pop-up menu, you’ll click on Customize Toolbar.
![img](http://media.idownloadblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/OS-X-Finder-Customize-Toolbar-Button.png)
* 3) A new interface appears with a variety of different buttons and functionality features that you can drag and drop into the toolbar.
![img](http://media.idownloadblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/Finder-Toolbar-Editing-Interface-OS-X.png)
* 4) Select something to add or remove from the Finder toolbar, and then click and drag it in or out of it; Example: Share button.
![img](http://media.idownloadblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/Editing-the-Finder-toolbar-drag-and-drop.png)
* 5) Click on the blue Done button to save any changes you make.
* And now you’ve just customized your Finder toolbar. That was easy!
* Customizing the Finder toolbar is something every Mac user should know how to do, and it can help improve workflow productivity depending on how you use your Mac.
* If you’re afraid you’ll mess something up, or you want to ever go back to the factory settings for the Finder toolbar, you can always drag the default set back into the toolbar, as shown at the bottom of the toolbar-editing interface.
