* Gmail is one of the most powerful third-party email clients by far; even many Apple device users have Gmail accounts.
* One of the useful things you can do right from Gmail is create rules via filters, and this lets you do all sorts of things with your email inbox for customizing your experience. We’ll show you how to do that in this tutorial.

* As you can see, the list is quite extensive. One of my favorite reasons to use rules via filters in Gmail is to automatically delete messages from annoying people so I don’t have to later on.
* If any of the above functions look appealing to you, then you’re probably itching to know how to do it. Here are the steps:
* 1) Log into Gmail from your favorite web browser and open your inbox.
* 2) In the search bar, click on the drop-down arrow.
![img](http://media.idownloadblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Gmail-Drop-Down-Arrow.png)
* 3) Now enter your search criteria to filter by:
![img](http://media.idownloadblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Gmail-Search-Criteria.png)
* It’s typically good to filter by a specific email address, but you can also filter by any of the following:
* 4) Once satisfied with what you’re filtering by, click on the blue Magnifying Glass button at the bottom left.
* 5) Now you will have to choose what happens to emails that meet your filter criteria. This is known as setting rules:
![img](http://media.idownloadblog.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/10/Filter-Type-Gmail.png)
* 6) Make your rule selection based on what you want to do, then click on the blue Create Filter button at the bottom left.
* Now any time you send or receive emails that meet your filter criteria, your rules will be applied to those email messages.
* Even if you’re using Apple’s Mail app instead of the Gmail app or website on your device, your rules will be applied before they even hit your inbox in the Mail app, so this is an incredibly useful feature to know.
